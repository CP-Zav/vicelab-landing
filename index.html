01 — Context

The Challenge

Large festivals bring together thousands of people in high-energy environments where heat, crowd density, fatigue, and substance use can intersect.

While organisers work hard to manage safety, many risks emerge dynamically between attendees, conditions, and timing.

Supporting safer decisions requires better information, clearer signals, and tools designed for real festival environments.

🌡
Heat, crowd density, and fatigue create compounding risk factors that evolve throughout an event.
⚡
Risks often emerge dynamically — between people, conditions, and timing — not just from single causes.
🛡
Better information and clearer signals give attendees and organisers more time to act safely.
02 — Architecture

The Ecosystem

The project is structured as three connected layers.

Layer 01
ViceLab
Research and knowledge development focused on harm-reduction science, interaction awareness, and evidence-based safety communication.
Layer 02
Cooked Pilot
Attendee-focused safety tools designed for festival environments, helping people stay aware of conditions, interactions, and support options.
Layer 03
VibeGuard
Organiser safety intelligence designed to support event teams with insights, compliance awareness, and emerging risk signals.

Together these layers connect research, technology, and event operations into a single safety ecosystem.

03 — Purpose

Why This Matters

Festivals are powerful cultural spaces. They bring people together to share music, art, exploration, and moments that can stay with us for a lifetime.

Supporting safer environments protects both the experience and the community that makes these events possible.

By combining research, attendee awareness tools, and organiser intelligence, this ecosystem aims to strengthen the foundations that keep festival culture thriving.

Because the best nights should end with great stories, not unnecessary risks.
