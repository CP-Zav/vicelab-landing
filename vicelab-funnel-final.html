02 — Lead Magnet → Google Doc or Canva → export PDF → attach in MailerLite email
TITLE
ViceLab Field Brief 5 Things to Know Before the Night Starts
INTRO LINE
This is not a safety pamphlet. It is a pre-event reference — short enough to read in five minutes, specific enough to actually use.
SECTION 01
01 — BASELINE FIRST Before the night starts, note your actual baseline. Energy level, hydration, food, sleep deficit, any existing anxieties. Your baseline shapes how everything else lands. People who skip this lose their reference point by hour three — and have no frame for what "off" actually looks like.
SECTION 02
02 — SET A CREW CHECK-IN SIGNAL Agree on one signal before you go in — a word, a hand gesture, a specific question. "How's the vibe?" means something different than "You good?" Use it consistently. It creates a shared language for checking in without drawing attention or interrupting the moment.
SECTION 03
03 — KNOW THE EARLY BODY SIGNALS Altered states compress perception. The early signals of something going wrong — elevated heart rate, teeth clenching, unusual heat, reduced verbal coherence — often feel fine from the inside. They are more reliably visible from outside. Watch each other, not just yourself.
SECTION 04
04 — AGREE ON AN ESCALATION POINT BEFORE YOU NEED ONE The question "when do we get help?" should not be answered in the moment. Set a simple threshold in advance: if X happens, we move. If someone cannot communicate clearly, we move. Clarity now removes the decision overhead when it matters most.
SECTION 05
05 — LOCATION LOGIC Know two things before you go deep into any space: where the medic point is, and the fastest route out of the main area. This takes sixty seconds at entry. Most people never do it. In a situation requiring fast movement, those sixty seconds become the difference between a structured exit and a chaotic one.
